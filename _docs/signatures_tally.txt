 This will change from time to time, but, will describe
 where the actual signatures used by phpMussel
 originate from, either in whole or as derived. Again,
 these are calculated via quick and dirty line counts,
 don't include hardcoded signatures or checks, and may
 not necessarily always be 100% precise.

ClamAV <http://www.clamav.net/>                           394,720        -9,019
 This constitutes the main bulk of phpMussel's
 signatures and covers every signature type supported
 by phpMussel at this time.

PhishTank <http://www.phishtank.com/>                      14,414          +971
 Although in heavily modified and reduced form,
 phpMussel uses the PhishTank CSV of known phishing
 URLs to populate the main bulk of its standard mail
 scanning signatures.

SecuriteInfo <http://www.securiteinfo.com/>                13,540        +1,881
 SecuriteInfo.com provides a range of good optional
 supplementary MD5 signatures for the ClamAV engine.
 PhpMussel adapts a selection of those for within its
 "complex extended" signatures functionality.

NLNetLabs <http://nlnetlabs.nl/>                            1,440          +792
 Primarily concerning BATs and PE files, phpMussel has
 adapted a number of the '09 signatures available from
 NLNetLabs.

ZBB <http://www.spambotsecurity.com/>                          43            +1
 Adapted from signatures originally designed for use
 with ZB Block but with equal relevance to phpMussel.

phpMussel <http://sourceforge.net/projects/phpmussel/>      1,933           -19

 myself, based upon the research and direct analysis of
 malware samples and various other threats that I've
